- Existentialism, as seen in the works of Sartre and Dostoevsky, shares with the nihilism-transcendence concept a focus on grappling with meaninglessness, but existentialists do not see this as leading to transcendence, instead arguing that meaning is created through free will and conscious choice.
- Absurdism, exemplified in Camus' work, also starts from the premise that life is inherently meaningless, but rejects [transcendence](https://app.getrecall.ai/item/241a67d0-49d1-4626-b1f6-db1f39bfadde) as an escape from the absurd, instead embracing life's meaninglessness through continual confrontation and revolt.
- Naturalism, which holds that reality consists only of natural elements, rejects the nihilism-transcendence concept's talk of divine experiences as illusory, viewing any meaning as coming from human relationships, creativity, and morality, and seeing transcendence as a psychological phenomenon, not contact with a higher reality.
