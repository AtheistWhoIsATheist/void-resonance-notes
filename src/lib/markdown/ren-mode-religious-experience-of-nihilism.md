# REN Mode Structured Inquiry: Religious Experience of Nihilism

**Nihilism**: Philosophical stance asserting the absence of intrinsic meaning, purpose, or value in existence, including skepticism toward objective morality and ontological foundations. (36 words)

**Religious experience**: Intense, often transformative encounter with what a subject apprehends as ultimate reality, encompassing numinous, mystical, or apophatic modalities beyond ordinary cognition. (32 words)

**Existential dread**: Acute phenomenological awareness of contingency, finitude, and possible absurdity, manifesting as visceral recognition of meaninglessness and destabilized self-world coherence. (28 words)

## Section 1 — Nihilism Preface
- **Objective**: Map the ontological and epistemic commitments distinguishing Nihiltheistic (NT) analysis from existentialist responses to nihilism.
- **Guiding Questions**:
  - How does NT treat meaninglessness as apophatic aperture rather than crisis resolution, in contrast to existentialist value-creation programs?
  - Which historical inflection points reveal divergences between NT interpretive strategies and existentialist authenticity narratives?
  - What methodological safeguards prevent NT inquiry from defaulting to consolatory metaphysics?
- **Required Outputs**:
  - Comparative matrix of NT vs existentialism across ontology, praxis, and epistemology.
  - Annotated timeline of pivotal thinkers marking shifts in nihilism discourse toward religious phenomenology.
  - Paradox testing protocol ensuring every identified divergence is evaluated for counter-resonances or self-negating implications.
- **Wordcount Floor**: ≥700 words.
